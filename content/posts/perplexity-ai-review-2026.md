Perplexity AI has positioned itself as the search engine for the AI era — conversational, cited, and actually helpful. After six weeks of making it our primary search tool, here's what works and what doesn't.

## What Is Perplexity?

Perplexity is an AI-powered answer engine. You ask a question in natural language, and it returns a synthesized answer with source citations, follow-up questions, and relevant context — rather than a list of links.

It has two modes:
- **Quick** — Fast answers from web results
- **Pro (Copilot)** — Deeper research using reasoning models (GPT-4o, Claude, etc.)

## Pricing

| Plan | Price | Features |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Unlimited Quick searches, limited Pro searches |
| Pro | $20/month | 300+ Pro searches/day, file upload, image generation |

The free tier is genuinely useful for casual research. Pro unlocks the heavier-lifting models.

## Where Perplexity Excels

### 1. Research with Citations

This is Perplexity's defining strength. Every answer comes with numbered citations linking to source material. Click any citation to see the full source, assess credibility, and verify the claim.

For journalists, students, researchers, and anyone who needs to know where information comes from, this workflow is dramatically better than Google. You're not wading through a dozen potentially-biased blog posts to find one factual nugget.

### 2. Follow-Up Questions

Perplexity suggests relevant follow-up questions after each answer. Clicking one dives deeper into that angle. This creates a conversation-like research flow that often surfaces related angles you wouldn't have thought to search for.

### 3. Complex, Multi-Part Queries

"Compare the pricing, features, and limitations of Notion, Obsidian, and Logseq for personal knowledge management" — Google gives you a list of review articles. Perplexity gives you a concise, structured comparison with current pricing and trade-offs, ready to act on.

For complex queries that would normally require synthesizing information from 5-10 sources, Perplexity saves serious time.

### 4. Current Events

Perplexity indexes news in near real-time. When something happens — a product launch, a policy change, a major event — Perplexity can tell you about it quickly, with sources. This is an area where even ChatGPT sometimes lags due to training cutoffs.

## Where Perplexity Falls Short

### 1. Depth on Technical Topics

For highly specialized content — specific programming error messages, obscure academic papers, detailed technical documentation — Perplexity sometimes provides a surface-level synthesis that you'd still need to verify against primary sources.

We encountered this with a question about a specific Kubernetes networking configuration. Perplexity's answer was plausible-sounding but subtly wrong. We had to verify against the official docs anyway.

### 2. Citation Quality Varies

Not all citations are equal. Perplexity sometimes cites Wikipedia or low-authority blogs as sources for claims that deserve more rigorous sourcing. You can't blindly trust the citations without checking the quality of the source itself.

### 3. Limited Customization

Google Search has advanced operators (site:, filetype:, intitle:, etc.) for precise queries. Perplexity's search is more of a conversation — you describe what you want and hope the AI interprets correctly. There's less fine-grained control for power users.

### 4. Can Be Overly Confident

Like many AI tools, Perplexity sometimes presents synthesized answers with more certainty than the sources warrant. When sources disagree, Perplexity should present the disagreement, but often it picks one interpretation as definitive.

### 5. Pro Search Rate Limits

The 300+ Pro searches per day sounds generous, but during deep research sessions, you can hit the limit. Each Pro search triggers the more capable model, and they're metered. When you run out, you fall back to Quick searches which are noticeably less capable.

## Best Use Cases

**Perplexity is excellent for:**
- Starting research projects and getting oriented quickly
- Fact-checking with source verification
- Staying current on fast-moving topics
- Comparing products, services, or options
- Getting quick summaries of complex topics

**You still need Google for:**
- Specific technical documentation lookups
- Advanced search operators and precision queries
- Local business information (maps, reviews, hours)
- Shopping with price comparisons
- Navigating to known websites

## Final Verdict

Perplexity AI is the most useful AI search tool we've used. It genuinely accelerates research and provides a better starting point than a raw Google search for most knowledge-work questions.

We don't use it exclusively — Google still wins for specific lookups, local info, and precision queries. But for "I want to understand X" or "What are the leading options for Y?", Perplexity is now our first stop.

At $20/month for Pro, it's worth it if you do research-heavy work. The free tier is sufficient for casual users.

**Rating: 8/10** — Excellent for research tasks, occasionally unreliable for technical depth.
