As a solopreneur, I'm simultaneously the CEO, accountant, marketer, content creator, customer support, and janitor. AI tools have become essential multipliers — they don't replace me, but they let me do the work of several people.

After 18 months of testing and refining my tools, here's the stack I've settled on. Every tool earns its place by either saving substantial time or enabling work I couldn't do alone.

## My Core Stack (Daily Use)

### 1. ChatGPT Plus ($20/month) — The General Brain

ChatGPT is my first thinking partner for most tasks:

- Drafting client emails and proposals
- Brainstorming content ideas and angles
- Quick coding questions and debugging
- Image generation for social media
- Translating complex ideas into simple language
- Voice mode for thinking through problems on walks

**Time saved:** 8-10 hours/week across tasks

### 2. Claude Pro ($20/month) — The Precision Thinker

When I need accurate, nuanced analysis:

- Long-form blog posts and guides
- Technical documentation
- Complex client reports requiring careful reasoning
- Analyzing lengthy documents or contracts
- Code generation for complex projects

**Time saved:** 5-7 hours/week, especially on writing tasks

### 3. Perplexity Pro ($20/month) — The Research Engine

For everything that needs research:

- Market research for new client proposals
- Staying current on industry trends
- Finding statistics and source material
- Competitor analysis
- Quickly getting oriented on unfamiliar topics

**Time saved:** 3-5 hours/week vs. manual Google research

### 4. Cursor ($20/month) — The Development Partner

For all coding and technical work:

- Building client websites and tools
- Automating repetitive tasks with scripts
- Debugging technical issues
- Creating custom integrations

**Time saved:** 10-15 hours/week on development tasks

## Content Creation Stack

### 5. Canva Pro ($13/month) — Visual Content

For all visual assets:

- Social media graphics and carousels
- Client presentation decks
- Email templates and newsletters
- Ad creatives for paid campaigns
- Blog post featured images

**Time saved:** 4-6 hours/week on visual content

### 6. ElevenLabs ($22/month) — Audio Content

For audio production:

- Podcast production (my voice cloned for corrections)
- Course narration
- Video voice-overs
- Audio versions of written content

**Time saved:** 6-8 hours/week for audio/video content

### 7. Descript ($33/month) — Video Content

For video editing:

- YouTube content editing
- Podcast post-production
- Course video creation
- Client testimonial and interview videos

**Time saved:** 8-10 hours/week on video editing

## Productivity Stack

### 8. Notion Team Plan + AI ($18+10/month) — Knowledge Base

My business brain:

- Client project management
- Content calendar and ideation
- Meeting notes and follow-ups
- Process documentation
- Finance tracking

**Time saved:** 5-7 hours/week on organization and retrieval

### 9. Fathom (Free for personal) — Meeting Intelligence

For every call:

- Automatic transcription and summaries
- Action items extraction
- Follow-up email drafts
- Searchable meeting history

**Time saved:** 2-3 hours/week on meeting admin

### 10. Zapier ($20/month) — Automation

Connecting everything:

- CRM updates from emails
- Social media scheduling
- Lead capture and routing
- Invoice generation
- Notification workflows

**Time saved:** 5-7 hours/week on manual coordination

## Workflow Examples

### New Client Onboarding
1. **Research:** Perplexity researches client's industry
2. **Proposal:** Claude drafts proposal based on research and my notes
3. **Polish:** ChatGPT refines tone and language
4. **Visuals:** Canva creates branded deck
5. **Capture:** Notion AI logs everything in client workspace

### Content Production Day
1. **Research:** Perplexity finds source material
2. **Draft:** Claude writes long-form article
3. **Edit:** Grammarly catches issues
4. **Visuals:** Canva creates featured image
5. **Audio:** ElevenLabs generates audio version
6. **Video:** Descript creates video version

### Client Call Workflow
1. **Before:** Perplexity researches client's recent news
2. **During:** Fathom transcribes live
3. **After:** ChatGPT drafts follow-up email
4. **Capture:** Zapier logs meeting notes to Notion

## Key Principles

1. **Pick tools that integrate, not just excel** — A tool that's 80% as good but integrates with everything else saves time overall.

2. **Automate routine, not judgment** — Use AI for repetitive mechanical tasks. Keep human judgment for client-facing decisions.

3. **Budget for quality** — Free tiers are fine for testing. Paid plans justify themselves if saving >1 hour/week.

4. **Review stack quarterly** — AI tools evolve fast. Reassess what's still worth paying for.

## Final Thoughts

$200/month of AI tools enables a solopreneur to deliver work quality and volume that previously required a small team. The ROI is extraordinary if you're actually using them daily.

The key isn't having every tool — it's embedding a core set deeply into your workflow until they become as natural as email.
