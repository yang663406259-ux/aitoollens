Gamma.app takes a different approach to presentations: instead of slide-by-slide editing, you describe what you want to create and AI generates a full presentation. Then you refine and customize.

Does this actually work? After creating 15+ decks across business pitches, internal updates, client proposals, and conference talks, here's our assessment.

## TL;DR

Gamma is genuinely useful for quickly producing presentable decks, especially when you're creating presentations you don't need perfect control over. It shines for internal updates, client proposals, and pitches that need to be done fast. For highly-polished design or brand-specific presentations, dedicated design tools still win. At $16/month, Gamma offers good value for frequent presentation creators.

## How Gamma Works

### 1. Generation
Describe your presentation topic and context. Gamma generates a full outline, then fills in content across slides.

### 2. Customization
Edit individual slides, swap layouts, change themes, and refine content. The interface is more like a website editor than a traditional slide tool, which is both a strength and limitation.

### 3. Presentation
Present directly from Gamma, record narration, export to PDF/PPTX, or share as a web-based deck with viewers.

## Where Gamma Excels

### 1. Speed
From concept to presentable deck in under 10 minutes. The AI generates reasonable structure and content that you can then refine. For internal updates or first drafts, this speed is game-changing.

### 2. Content First, Design Second
Gamma's web-like format prioritizes content over pixel-perfect design. Text flows well, layouts adapt to content, and you don't waste time aligning elements precisely.

### 3. Web-Based Presentations
Shared Gamma decks are web-based with smooth scrolling, embedded media, and interactive elements. For remote audiences viewing asynchronously, this format works better than static slides.

### 4. Refinement Loop
AI can regenerate individual sections, suggest alternative phrasings, or expand bullet points. Iterating is fast compared to redesigning slides manually.

### 5. Import and Repurpose
Convert existing documents (Google Docs, Notion pages, PDFs) into presentations with one click. Good for repurposing content across formats.

## Where Gamma Falls Short

### 1. Design Ceiling
Gamma's templates are consistent and attractive, but they all feel like Gamma. Creating truly bespoke, brand-specific designs is limited. You can customize colors, fonts, and logos, but the fundamental layouts remain Gamma-flavored.

### 2. Limited Animation Options
Gamma doesn't support slide-by-slide build animations like PowerPoint offers. For presentations that rely heavily on progressive reveal or complex animation sequences, Gamma isn't sufficient.

### 3. Export Limitations
While PDF and PPTX export exist, exported files lose Gamma's interactive features and sometimes formatting quirks. Presenting natively in Gamma is superior but requires viewers to load a web page.

### 4. Enterprise Gaps
For organizations with established slide template standards (most Fortune 500s), Gamma doesn't match existing brand guidelines precisely. Adobe or PowerPoint still wins for corporate templates.

### 5. Occasional AI Misfires
AI-generated content sometimes includes inaccurate statistics, generic filler, or off-topic tangents. Reviewing generated content is essential — can't trust it on autopilot.

## Real Use Cases

**Executive weekly internal update:** Excellent. Gamma handles the content-first approach well, and viewers consume on web.

**Startup pitch to investors:** Good. Polished enough for most pitch scenarios, fast iteration on different versions.

**Conference keynote:** Decent for smaller conferences. For major keynotes with tight production values, still need PowerPoint/Keynote.

**Client proposal:** Very good. Fast to customize per client, web-based viewing feels modern.

**Sales enablement materials:** Mixed. Good for individual reps but not enterprise-wide standardization.

## Final Verdict

Gamma is a genuine time-saver for anyone creating multiple presentations per month, especially when maximum design polish isn't the priority. It's excellent for internal communications, client proposals, and quick pitches.

At $16/month for Plus, Gamma is worth it if you're creating 2+ presentations monthly. The free tier is generous enough to test thoroughly before committing.

**Rating: 8/10** — Fast, effective AI presentation tool, limited mainly by design ceiling and enterprise needs.
