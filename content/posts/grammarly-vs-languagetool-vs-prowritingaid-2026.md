AI writing editors go beyond basic spell-check. They understand context, improve clarity, adjust tone, and catch subtle errors that traditional tools miss. But the three leading options — Grammarly, LanguageTool, and ProWritingAid — each shine in different areas.

After testing each on hundreds of pieces of content across different writing scenarios, here's our honest comparison.

## Quick Verdict

- **Grammarly:** Best for general business writing and ease of use
- **LanguageTool:** Best for multilingual writers and privacy-conscious users
- **ProWritingAid:** Best for creative writers and those wanting in-depth analysis

## Pricing

| | Grammarly | LanguageTool | ProWritingAid |
|---|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| **Free** | Basic checks | 10,000 chars/check | Limited checks |
| **Paid (individual)** | $12/month | $8.33/month | $10/month |
| **Lifetime option** | No | No | $79 (one-time) |
| **Team** | $15/member/month | Custom | $15/member/month |

ProWritingAid's lifetime deal ($79 once, $10/month equivalent over ~6 months) is the best value over time.

## Grammar Checks and Error Detection

All three catch standard grammar and spelling errors reliably. Differences emerge on subtle issues:

**Grammarly:** Excellent at contextual grammar — distinguishing "its" vs "it's" based on sentence structure. Occasionally over-corrects stylistic choices.

**LanguageTool:** Strong accuracy with good contextual understanding. Open-source rules engine means community-contributed checks for edge cases.

**ProWritingAid:** Most comprehensive check list. Catches issues other tools miss, including overused words, echo, and sentence variety problems. Sometimes flags stylistic choices aggressively.

**Winner:** ProWritingAid for comprehensiveness; Grammarly for accuracy-to-annoyance ratio.

## Style and Clarity Suggestions

**Grammarly:** Clean suggestions that improve readability without being intrusive. Tone detection (formal, friendly, confident) is useful. "Writing goals" feature lets you set audience, formality, intent, and emotion — then adjusts suggestions accordingly.

**LanguageTool:** Fewer style suggestions but solid fundamentals. Picky Readability feature highlights complex sentences and passive voice. Less opinionated than competitors.

**ProWritingAid:** Deep analysis with 20+ reports covering readability, pacing, dialogue, diction, and more. The most sophisticated style feedback of any tool. Particularly powerful for longer writing projects.

**Winner:** ProWritingAid for depth; Grammarly for balanced, non-intrusive help.

## Creative Writing Support

**Grammarly:** Basic support. Good for clarity but not specifically tuned for fiction or creative nonfiction.

**LanguageTool:** Minimal creative writing features. Good for basic error catching in novels.

**ProWritingAid:** Excellent for fiction and creative work. Specific reports for dialogue tags, pacing, sensory language, and showing vs. telling. Used by professional authors and recognized for deep creative writing support.

**Winner:** ProWritingAid, clearly.

## Multilingual Support

**Grammarly:** English only (US, UK, Australia, Canada). No other languages.

**LanguageTool:** Supports 30+ languages including Spanish, French, German, Portuguese, Dutch, Russian. Each language has its own rule sets and native speakers contributing.

**ProWritingAid:** English only (multiple dialects).

**Winner:** LanguageTool, unchallenged.

## Privacy and Data Handling

**Grammarly:** Cloud-based processing. Grammarly reads everything you type in supported apps. Enterprise plans offer enhanced controls.

**LanguageTool:** Two modes — cloud-based (like Grammarly) or self-hosted (all processing stays local). Self-hosted option is unique and appeals to privacy-conscious users.

**ProWritingAid:** Cloud-based processing with enterprise options.

**Winner:** LanguageTool, with its self-hosted option.

## Integrations

**Grammarly:** Most extensive. Browser extensions (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge), Microsoft Office, Google Docs, desktop apps for Mac/Windows, mobile keyboards.

**LanguageTool:** Browser extensions, Microsoft Office, Google Docs, desktop apps. Good coverage but fewer integrations than Grammarly.

**ProWritingAid:** Browser extensions, Microsoft Office, Google Docs, Scrivener integration (important for writers), desktop apps.

**Winner:** Grammarly for breadth; ProWritingAid for specific creative writing integrations (Scrivener).

## User Interface

**Grammarly:** Cleanest, most polished interface. Intuitive, fast, and unobtrusive. Suggestions appear contextually with clear explanations.

**LanguageTool:** Functional interface, slightly less polished. Web editor is decent. Browser extension occasionally slow.

**ProWritingAid:** Most complex interface, reflecting its more comprehensive feature set. Learning curve to understand all reports. Desktop app is particularly powerful but requires exploration.

**Winner:** Grammarly for UX; ProWritingAid for power users.

## Best Use Cases

**Grammarly:**
- Business emails and professional communication
- Blog posts and marketing copy
- Student academic writing
- General everyday writing
- Users wanting simple, effective help

**LanguageTool:**
- Multilingual writers
- Privacy-conscious users (self-hosted option)
- Developers (extensive API)
- Open-source advocates
- Users wanting a free alternative with good quality

**ProWritingAid:**
- Novelists and creative writers
- Long-form content creators
- Academic researchers
- Users wanting in-depth style analysis
- Writers with one-time budget (lifetime deal)

## Final Recommendations

**For most people:** Grammarly is the default choice. It's polished, comprehensive enough for typical needs, and works everywhere.

**For creative writers:** ProWritingAid offers depth that Grammarly can't match. The lifetime deal makes it exceptional value.

**For multilingual or privacy-focused users:** LanguageTool is the obvious choice, especially with self-hosting.

**Budget option:** LanguageTool's free tier is genuinely useful. ProWritingAid's lifetime deal is best long-term value.
